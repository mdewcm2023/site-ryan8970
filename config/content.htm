Web-based content management systems (CMSs) provide a user-friendly interface for creating, editing, and publishing digital content on websites. These systems allow multiple users to collaborate on content creation and management, making it easier to keep your website up-to-date and relevant. In this course, you will learn about the key features of web-based CMSs, including content creation and editing tools, user management and permissions, and workflow management. You will also explore the different types of CMSs available, including open-source and proprietary systems, and learn how to evaluate and select the right CMS for your needs. Through hands-on exercises and practical examples, you will gain the skills and knowledge you need to effectively manage your web content using a web-based CMS.
